  • Embarassing Story?

    •  Well, this is a bit of a long one, and to be honest, I'm certain other people might not even find it embarassing. I was competing in a math competition in seventh grade - my team had made it to the state level. After we took a bunch of tests (yes, we literally just sat down and took tests for fun), there was a quickfire questions round which was a one-on-one competition for the top eight scores on the tests. I was 6th place coming into those rounds, so after 7 beat 8, it was my time to go against him. I got in front of everyone - a little nervous - and was we were given our buzzers and then they asked us a really silly question:
      What positive, whole intiger, when divided by its negative reciprocol, becomes -9
       Okay, now you might no even know that answer from reading it, but if you do, imagine trying to work that out in your head when about a hundred people are watching you. I totally blanked on what a reciprocal was. The guy who was in 7th buzzes in and says -3. Man that was so close to the right answer and if I wasn't both nervous and dumbfounded I would have absolutely retorted with "The answer is 3", but I just stood there wide-eyed as the proctor said "do you have an answer?"
       I shot off a fairly quick -1/3, then immediately realized they said positive whole integer which meant not only was starting with negative wrong, but I took what the other guy did a step further and made it a fraction.
       Like I said, you probably don't find that embarassing, but I took great pride in those competitions and as the youngest person at the event I felt like I really showed my inexperience.
